Our Mission

Our mission works towards a future where wild wolves and wolf-dogs are safe and where women are empowered. Our sanctuary rescues wolf-dogs and provides lifelong care and education. Founded in 2013 in Northern California, Women for Wolves started as a movement to raise awareness for wildlife. Over the past decade, we‚Äôve empowered 50+ women in conservation & rescue, led 30+ youth programs, and reached 180,000+ people through education and advocacy.

Women for Wolves is a non-profit organization that fights to protect wild wolves and provide sanctuary for wolf-dogs. We are a 501(c)3 non-profit, wolf-dog sanctuary, and a USDA Licensed Facility. We are not open to the public currently, but check our events page for upcoming events or hikes with our rescues.

Our women-led staff gets in the trenches to save wolf-dogs from horrific abuse and neglect.

  • 40+ Youth groups educated.
  • 35+ Bills with advocacy support.
  • 24 Wolf-dogs & Northern Breed dogs at our sanctuary.
  • 5,000,000+ People reached each month.
