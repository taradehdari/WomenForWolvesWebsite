COEXISTENCE FOR ALL

Women for Wolves protects wolves & wildlife and empowers the feminine ‚Äî restoring balance to the Earth through compassion, education, and coexistence.

Over 1 million wolves were killed across the United States through government-funded extermination campaigns that mirrored the broader oppression of the wild ‚Äî and of women and Indigenous peoples. Coexistence, community, and nature have been silenced under systems that value profit over balance.

Women for Wolves was founded to change that. Our mission is to amplify diverse voices in conservation through grants, advocacy, rescue, and partnerships that unite science, storytelling, and spirituality in defense of the wild.

In conservation today, women remain vastly underrepresented ‚Äî with less than 3% of global environmental leadership roles held by women ‚Äî while Indigenous perspectives continue to be excluded from wildlife management decisions. Meanwhile, wolves remain persecuted: over 1,400 wolves were legally killed in the U.S. between 2021 and 2024, and up to 90% of captive wolf-dogs are euthanized. Our approach reimagines conservation as collaboration. We empower women, Indigenous groups, and local communities to lead coexistence efforts that protect apex predators and restore the ecosystems we all depend on.

In an industry where women are outnumbered by men 100:1 at the operational level, Women for Wolves places women in authentic leadership roles and builds pathways for the next generation of female conservationists ‚Äî while safeguarding a future for wild wolves.

Through our programs, Women for Wolves contributes to:

  • Wild wolf recovery in California, where populations have rebounded to approximately 45 wolves across 7 active packs under new state protections.
  • Wolf protection policy and advocacy at state and federal levels, supporting legislation like H.R. 4754 and AB 1322 to ban trapping and reinforce non-lethal management.
  • Women‚Äôs empowerment in conservation, having trained and supported 50+ women leaders and reached 8 million people monthly through education and advocacy.

Our agenda: to end wolf trapping and hunting nationwide, reform outdated wildlife management systems, and restore wolves to their rightful ecological role as keystone species.

How we are making an impact.

  • 300+ Wolf-dogs & animals rescued.
  • 60+ Women empowered with grants or conservation/animal behavior careers.
  • 40+ Youth groups educated.
  • 35+ Bills with advocacy support.
  • 24 Wolf-dogs & Northern Breed dogs at our sanctuary.
  • 5,000,000+ People reached each month.
